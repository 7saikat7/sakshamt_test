Who are we ?

Who are we ?

We are Saksham. A non-profit organization which aims to create a positive social impact by protecting and restoring Prison Rights in India, accomplishing necessary reforms in prison administration, and rehabilitating former prisoners.

A bit of history

A bit of history

A group of legal professionals came together in 2018 to set up Saksham, working at the grass-roots level to initiate positive changes in the lives of prisoners. In India 80 percent of the inmates in jail are under trials; they are not granted bails, their rights are violated and they are subjected to oppression in jail. They live under harsh conditions with negligible medical facilities. Though they are entitled to rights through Prisoners Act, 1894; the Model Manual Prison India, etc, the problem is that they are not effectively implemented. A very large number of underâ€“trial prisoners suffer prolonged incarceration even in petty criminal matters merely because they are not in a position, even in bailable offenses, to furnish bail bonds and get released on bail. It is an undeniable fact that the poor, illiterate and weaker sections in our country suffer day in and day out in their struggle for survival and look up to those who have promised them equality- social, political and economic.

Our Vision

Our vision

Prisoners are generally marginalized by society and labeled as offenders. Their plight is usually overlooked to the extent that they end up losing even the basic rights as human beings. Saksham, as the name suggests, will strive to bring hope and justice to prisoners, former prisoners and their families by making them self-reliant so that they become valued members of our society. Our vision is to impact positive social change so that justice, which has become a distant dream, turns into reality. The initiative which is yet to kick-start, hopes to evolve and spread its wings in various cities across India.

Our Mission

Our Mission
  • To serve the Prison Department by extending support to prisoners, former prisoners and their families in the field of counseling, health, education, vocational training, rehabilitation, culture therapy, yoga, meditation, drug de-addiction, legal-aid, and moral upliftment
  • To work in collaboration with government to address issues of overcrowding and merciless conditions
  • To provide direct assistance where we can, to improve access to clean water, better sanitation, emergency medical assistance, food, security, clothing and general infrastructure
  • To give a second chance to the less educated, underprivileged youth by generating employment based on urban and rural livelihood training provided in partnership with stakeholders
  • To provide special outreach to the children of prisoners who are often the forgotten victims of crime
  • Additionally we will work on victim -offender reconciliation and rigorously advocate Restorative Justice
